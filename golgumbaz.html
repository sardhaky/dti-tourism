Gol Gumbaz Restoration Timeline

Overview

Gol Gumbaz, located in Bijapur, Karnataka, is a mausoleum built in the 17th century for Sultan Muhammad Adil Shah. It is known for its massive dome, which is one of the largest in the world.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

17th Century

The Gol Gumbaz is constructed by Sultan Muhammad Adil Shah as his tomb, showcasing the architectural brilliance of the Deccan Sultanates.

19th Century

The tomb experiences some deterioration due to neglect and environmental factors, and initial restoration work is carried out by the British colonial authorities.

20th Century

The Archaeological Survey of India begins systematic restoration to preserve the monument's integrity, including the dome and its unique acoustics.

21st Century

Further restoration and preservation efforts continue to address wear and tear caused by weathering and to ensure the long-term preservation of the monument.
