Ajanta Caves Restoration Timeline

Overview

The Ajanta Caves, located in Maharashtra, are a group of Buddhist rock-cut caves and temples, adorned with murals and sculptures, dating back to the 2nd century BCE to 480 CE.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

2nd Century BCE

The Ajanta Caves are initially excavated and painted by Buddhist monks as places of worship and meditation.

5th Century CE

The caves are abandoned, and they remain lost to history for centuries.

19th Century

The caves are rediscovered by a British officer, and early restoration efforts are initiated.

20th Century

Extensive restoration work is carried out to preserve the paintings and sculptures, supported by Indian and international conservationists.
