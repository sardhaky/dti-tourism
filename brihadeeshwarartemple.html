Brihadeeswarar Temple Restoration Timeline

Overview

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, is one of the largest temples in India, built by the Chola dynasty in the 11th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

11th Century

The temple is constructed by Raja Raja Chola I and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

16th Century

The temple undergoes additions and renovations by the Nayak rulers of Thanjavur.

19th Century

British colonial administration starts preservation work on the temple, including repair of the Vimana (tower).

21st Century

In recent years, the Archaeological Survey of India carries out extensive restoration, including cleaning and preserving the murals and stone carvings.
