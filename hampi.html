Hampi Restoration Timeline

Overview

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and is known for its magnificent ruins, including temples, palaces, and market streets.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

14th Century

The city of Hampi is established and becomes the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire under King Harihara I.

16th Century

Hampi flourishes with the construction of grand temples and monuments, including the Virupaksha Temple and the Vittala Temple.

1565

Hampi is sacked during the Battle of Talikota, leading to the destruction of many structures.

20th Century

Restoration efforts begin, especially after Hampi is declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986. The Archaeological Survey of India leads conservation work on the temples and structures.
