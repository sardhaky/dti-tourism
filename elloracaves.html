Ellora Caves Restoration Timeline

Overview

The Ellora Caves are a complex of rock-cut temples and monasteries, located in Maharashtra, India. They date back to between the 5th and 10th centuries CE.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

5th-10th Century CE

The caves are carved, with Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain temples built into the cliffs.

17th Century

The caves fall into neglect as the region faces political instability and invasion.

19th Century

The caves are rediscovered by the British, who begin efforts to document and preserve them.

20th Century

Extensive restoration work begins under the Archaeological Survey of India to preserve the intricate carvings and sculptures.
