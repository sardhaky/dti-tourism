Rani ki Vav Restoration Timeline

Overview

Rani ki Vav, located in Patan, Gujarat, is a stepwell built in the 11th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

11th Century

Rani ki Vav is constructed by Queen Udayamati in memory of her husband, King Bhimdev I.

14th Century

The stepwell is abandoned as the region faces invasions and decline.

19th Century

Rani ki Vav is rediscovered, and initial conservation efforts are made to clear the well of debris.

21st Century

The Archaeological Survey of India undertakes comprehensive restoration to preserve the carvings and structural integrity of the stepwell.
