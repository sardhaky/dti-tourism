Khajuraho Temples Restoration Timeline

Overview

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments in Madhya Pradesh, India, is famous for its stunning temples adorned with intricate sculptures, including erotic art, dating from 950 to 1150 CE.

Timeline of Key Events and Restoration Efforts

10th-12th Century

The temples are constructed by the Chandela Dynasty and are renowned for their architectural beauty and artistic sculptures.

14th Century

Following the decline of the Chandela dynasty, the temples fall into neglect and are abandoned.

19th Century

The temples are rediscovered by the British, and initial conservation efforts are made, although many structures remain in disrepair.

20th Century

The Archaeological Survey of India undertakes large-scale restoration and preservation work to protect the temples and sculptures.
